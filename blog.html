is cryptography impactful?

tamara tayeb / july 23rd, 2021

Without cryptography the world would not be as modern as it is, Cryptography is all around us, quite literally, this blog is being read through an encrypted website!

Isn’t it interesting to realize that we have been using cryptography all our lives, most of the time we ordinary people use cryptology in a more straightforward manner, versus the very complex manner, which is found more often in websites and used in big corporations, etc.

It all started in us, from having a secret word between two best friends, then having to create your email password, the bank password, etc., most of those required cryptography, as many people replace their words with numbers or change up a word that has significance to them to make it encrypted.

Cryptography is for the most part safe and positive. A couple facts may be: Data encryption keeps information distinct from the security of the device on which it is stored. Also, the data is secure regardless of how it is sent, since the encryption is on the data itself, plus, several organizations are required to comply with strict confidentiality requirements as well as other related regulations, data that has been encrypted can only be read by the recipient who possesses the key to decrypt it.

For example, a food company, the company would have recipes that are required to be secret, yet that recipe must be shared with the employees of the company. Here, the company could have the recipe encrypted therefore only the employees of that company will know what the recipe is, even if the note or paper or message of the recipe got to someone who was not working there, they would not know the recipe... smart I would say. In addition to that, it is a big company after all, it could have many important documents of how the company is run, important files of the employees, company transactions, etc. Most documents nowadays are in computers, if those documents are encrypted, no matter who may get access to the computer or documents they would not know the company secrets, as all their information has been encrypted.

Cryptography is a vital aspect that many of us never really noticed, including me. Cryptography has and will continue to evolve, we are all born with the skill of encryption, it just depends who is willing to grow that talent and who is willing to let it die.
